Atletico de Madrid

The story of almost any ever known Disney movie. The story of a small, defenseless character, which always had a lot to offer but had bad luck throughout life. The Treasure Planet, El Dorado, Toy Story, Finding Nemo... Atletico de Madrid. A team which in the year 1996 won a double, and three years later got relegated to the second division, along with many institutional problems. Couldn't come back to the first division after the first year, but that was when Fernando "El Nino" Torres came into play, being only 18 years old. With him and the help of a team that had lost it's soul, and wasn't sure where it was going, we came back to the first division. Years went by, Atletico was becoming a pretty solid mid-table team but had a problem: One day we would beat Barcelona 4-3 (On a season which they only lost one game, this one) and the next, we would get kicked out of the Copa del Rey against a Segunda B team. You never knew what was going to happen. More years went by, great players came and went, but something was cooking. In between those dark years we happen upon a UEFA Europa League title. Very important win in a Final against Fulham, the Diego Forlan days... And with that, a Europe Supercup title. But then a decision that would change the club's history was made. It was around Christmas of 2011, Atletico had been kicked out of Copa del Rey by a second divission team, and were 4 points from relegation in the league. Still alive in the Europa League. And that's when it happened. Diego Pablo Simeone, new Atletico de Madrid manager. I could go on and on about how he changed everything in the club and we became a real competitive team. I'll try not to. I'll start with a number that sums up Simeone's work in the club: We hadn't beat Real Madrid once in the last 14 years. Since he came, we've won 6 out of 13 League and Copa del Rey matches. Add to that, a Uefa Europa League, a Europe Supercup, a Copa Del Rey, a Liga BBVA that hadn't been won by other team than Madrid or Barcelona in more than a decade, and reach a Champions League final. I'd go on and on, but that about sums the story of a broken team, that stood from the ashes, and is today tied in first place in the League with Barcelona, and is in the Champions League semifinals against Bayern Munich.
